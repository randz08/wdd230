Who We Are

Temple Inn & Suites is a hotel chain headquartered in Bethesda, MD that builds and manages specialized hotel accommodations near temple locations of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints around the world. This owner-operated company caters to temple workers, patrons, and their families by providing specialized services and accommodations to meet their needs. Our hotel accommodations and services are available to anyone willing to abide by our company's terms and policies, in order to maintain a clean and family-friendly atmosphere for our patrons.

We also offer many specialized services for those serving full time missions as temple workers, or those preparing to get married and/or sealed in a Temple near one of our locations. For more details, please take a look at Our Services

Featured - Manila Philippines Temple

Get 10% Off Any Stay In Our Featured Location

President Gordon B. Hinckley (1910–2008) dedicated the Manila Philippines Temple in September 1984. The beautiful six-spire building has an exterior of brilliant white ceramic tiles and is surrounded by majestic palm trees and colorful vegetation.

"The ordinances performed in our temples are vital to our salvation and to the salvation of our deceased loved onces. May we continue faithful in attending the temples, which are being built closer and closer to our members." -President Thomas S. Monson (October 2010 General Conference)

The temple’s open house was held shortly after two hurricanes ravaged the Philippines. Despite the storms, the events went on as planned. On September 3, 1984, a tour for dignitaries was held. On the following day, when the temple opened for public tours, “a beautiful heavenly spectacle was unfurled over the temple,” said Jovencio Ilagan, executive secretary of the temple committee during its construction. “The sun, in all its brilliance, was seen through a corona of varying colors. … At one point, the center spire with the statue of the Angel Moroni was seen at the center of the corona. Almost a hundred people at the temple grounds attest to it. Many were in tears.”

The Manila Philippines Temple was the first temple built in the Philippines. Construction on the country’s second temple began in 2007 in Cebu City.
